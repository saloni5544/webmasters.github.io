What is Our Mission

Our mission is to utilize the power of Artificial Intelligence in our drone known as the Eagle Bot which goes to disaster areas that are hard to reach for human rescue teams

Why

When you hear of some disaster such as the recent mudslides in Montecito, CA, wouldn't it be great if you could have drones that would work in all weather conditions, round the clock, and detect anyone alive or dead, deploy life-saving food/medicine or devices while alerting rescuers at some central coordination point as to the location by sending images? DroneAid allows us to assess the damage and detect any living or dead victims in an aftermath of a disaster such as the one reffered to above.

How

Technology such as ours has already been tested in Cape May, New Jersey where emergency supplies were delivered to a boat off the Jersey shore. In fact the United Nations supports the use of drones in disaster relief "[Drone delivery] allows for instant humanitarian relief within minutes and without having to worry about logistics". We believe that we can expand the use of this technology to empower communities with the peace of mind that help is indeed there in the event of a natural disaster.

When

We believe that with our close work with the FAA and with humanitarian and private relief organizations that we can make this technology accessible to communities across the nation very soon.

Thinking Ahead

Did you know that the Federal Aviation Administration began to hand out licenses for commercial drone operation. (There are at least 2,000 licensed pilots in the Houston area alone, and some 20,300 nationwide.)
